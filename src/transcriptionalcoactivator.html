Transcriptional co-activator: PGC-1a

<

PGC-1a regulates the oxidative metabolism of mitochondria, regulating glucose, lipid, and energy homeostasis (2,3). Expression of this gene in muscle fibers promotes resistance to fatigue, a trait very beneficial to athletes, while a lack of expression results in intolerance to exercise (2). The coactivators produced by this gene affect the body’s response to environmental and it’s reaction to temperature and nutritional status (2). PGC-1a works with other signals to change the expression in muscle fibres and altering the phenotype of the muscles (3).

An athletes ability to resist fatigue would increase their stamina as well as the amount of power they are capable of putting into their activity.
