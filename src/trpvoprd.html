Ultragenes

Genetic variation between sprint, endurance, and ultrarunners

Nervous System

Transient receptor potential V subtype 1 gene (TRPV1) & δ Opioid receptor subtype 1 gene (OPRD1)

The TRPV1 gene mediates the responses of sensory neurons to moderate thermal stimuli and OPRD1 codes for the murine δ opioid receptor(1). These two genes influence the metabolism of neurotransmitters, impacting individuals sensitivity to pain(1).

In an experiment by Kim et al. pain sensitivity and personality tests resembling those used by Freund et al. (2) were used to identify polymorphisms associated with pain perception. both Kim et al. and Freund et al. tested pain tolerance by using a cold withdrawal time (CWT) test and tested personality traits using a temperament and character inventory (TCL), Kim et al. additionally included SNP genotyping.

Alleles of the TRPV1 include Ile315 and Val585; homozygous Val585 individuals kept their hands in the cold water for an average 39.2 seconds longer than homozygous Ile315 (1). Individuals who scored low in harm avoidance during the TCL kept their hands in the water for an average of 40.9 seconds longer than those who scored high for harm avoidance (1). Comparing the results of these two tests would suggest that ultra runners are more likely to possess the Val585 allele at the TRPV1 locus (1,2). Individuals with C307C alleles at the OPRD1 locus phenotypically have a higher pain tolerance of heat while T307T alleles have a more sensitive heat pain tolerance (1).
